### 2.4 Recent Updates

2.4.1 NLI, zero-shot learning, BERT

Natural Language Inference (NLI) asks whether a hypothesis follows from a premise.

Outputs - Entailment — oes the second sentence make sense given the first one? (Yes) - Contradiction — Does the second sentence clearly not make sense? (No) - Neutral — Or is there not enough info to tell? (Maybe)

Example - First sentence: “A man is riding a bicycle.” - Second sentence: “A man is outdoors.” - Computer says: Yes — riding a bike usually means you’re outside. It is making an inference.

Why NLI matters

It’s a big deal because this is how computers start to think about meaning instead of just counting words. It helps with: - Fact-checking - Answering questions - Understanding rules and contracts - Helping customer service bots know what you mean —

Understanding Zero-Shot Learning

What is Zero-Shot?

Doing a new task without training data for that task.
We reframe the task as NLI: - Premise = the text to classify. - Hypotheses = each label written as a simple sentence.

The model picks the hypothesis that best fits.

Example — Zero-Shot Sentiment

  • Text: “The new café’s coffee is absolutely amazing!”
  • Hypotheses (labels as sentences):
    1. “This review is positive.”
    2. “This review is negative.”
    3. “This review is neutral.”
  • Prediction: Positive — no labeled dataset needed.

Models Used

Encoder-only transformers commonly used for NLI & zero-shot classification: - BERT — Bidirectional encoder; strong sentence understanding. - RoBERTa — Robustly optimized BERT; trained longer/better. - DeBERTa — Disentangled attention; strong NLI performance.

These models are good at understanding relationships between sentences, which is exactly what NLI needs.

This is new !

  • Before ~2020: You typically needed lots of labeled data.
  • Now: Strong NLI models enable zero-shot labeling for many practical tasks.
  • Result: Lower cost, faster pilots, and easier scaling of qualitative categories to large corpora.

Real-World Applications

  • Auto-tagging and organizing documents
  • Moderating harmful or misleading content
  • Routing customer messages to the right team

  • Rapidly exploring themes in survey/free-text responses
